Product Overview

Getting started coding for hardware is really hard. With kid-tested hardware and a groundbreaking learning platform, Awesome Shield teaches coding for hardware from the ground up. It’s never been easier to start coding with Arduino, the world’s preeminent tool for invention.

Awesome Shield is made up of three components:

  1. A video-based learning platform that is project based and self-directed.
  2. Simplified code that is easy to learn and optimized for growth.
  3. Plug-and-play hardware including a broad range of modular add-ons transform Awesome Shield from a foundational learning tool to a customizable platform for individual invention

But wait, there’s more...

  • With Awesome Shield, kids learn the science, technology, engineering, art, and math behind each and every lesson in coding for hardware projects. Interdisciplinary learning transforms tech education and provides context and appeal across a range of interests. With Awesome Shield, coders learn to build everything from toys and musical instruments to science experiments and sports trackers.
  • Awesome Shield is optimized for kids ages 11-17, but we’ve seen new coders from 8 to 80 learn and have fun with Awesome Shield.
  • Awesome Shield introduces coders to Arduino — a platform with unlimited potential, utilized by the very best in the hardware business.

Origin Story

The story of Awesome Shield begins like many Berlin startup stories do – with Beer. The three founders of Awesome Shield began working together on an automated system to help craft brewers maintain consistency. While prototyping with Arduino, our three intrepid co-founders found that manually setting up circuits and writing repetitive code was tiresome and buggy; so, to make prototyping faster, they created a circuit board with common sensors, buttons, switches, and a library of simplified code.

Over the winter holidays, Chris – Awesome Shield’s intrepid leader – showed his cousin the prototyping circuit board and the code he was writing, and pretty soon the first Awesome Shield protege was off and running with the basic skills he learned from that first circuit board and Chris’ coding instruction. A few months later, Chris got the call — his cousin had enrolled in a computer science degree. The time for beer had passed, now it was time to pivot.

Within months, Awesome Shield was born.

From one-on-one tutoring to public workshops, the feedback was clear from the get go — people love learning with Awesome Shield and bringing code to life through hardware. But the team was frustrated by how few people they could teach at a time, so now Awesome Shield is developing an online learning platform that will reach kids around the world and grow in real time to meet the needs and fuel the passions of young coders everywhere.

The three founders of Awesome Shield are Chris Palmer, Jon Amar, and Sebastian Himberger. Chris and Jon are brothers, and all three have been good friends since meeting in Berlin.
